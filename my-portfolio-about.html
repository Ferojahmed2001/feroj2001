About me

I’m a Bioinformatician, Computational Biologist, and Data Analyst with over two years of research experience in computational biology, bioinformatics, and AI-driven biomedical research. My work focuses on transforming complex biological data into meaningful scientific insights through statistical modeling, machine learning, and multi-omics data analysis.

With a strong background in Statistics, I specialize in RNA-seq, single-cell RNA sequencing, differential gene expression analysis, biomarker discovery, host-pathogen interaction analysis, immunoinformatics-driven vaccine design, and computational drug discovery. I have also contributed to immunoinformatics-driven vaccine design, integrating computational approaches to accelerate biomedical research.

I am proficient in Python, R, C++, Seurat, Bioconductor, Cytoscape, PyMOL, YASARA, and machine learning frameworks, enabling me to develop reproducible bioinformatics pipelines, analyze high-dimensional biological datasets, and build predictive models for precision medicine.

My research combines machine learning, explainable AI (XGBoost & SHAP), molecular docking, molecular dynamics simulation, and network biology to identify disease biomarkers, therapeutic targets, and potential drug candidates. I am particularly interested in precision medicine, computational drug discovery, single-cell transcriptomics, and AI-powered bioinformatics.

As the first author of a peer-reviewed publication in BioData Mining and a co-author of several publications in computational biology and bioinformatics, I enjoy collaborating with multidisciplinary research teams to solve challenging biological problems and translate data into meaningful discoveries.

Beyond research, I enjoy developing bioinformatics web tools, learning emerging technologies, and exploring innovative applications of artificial intelligence in healthcare. Feel free to explore my portfolio to learn more about my research, publications, and projects.
